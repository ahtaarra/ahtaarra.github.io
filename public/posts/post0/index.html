Simple Keyboard: a keyboard that doesn’t have to try

2023/02/08

Simple Keyboard is a keyboard app for Android OS based on the AOSP Keyboard. It is available on both the F-Droid repository and the Google Play Store. And needed, its APK can be directly grabbed from the project’s Github releases page here.

One of the reasons I moved to this keyboard had been that at the time I used to use Hacker’s Keyboard and wanted to try out something else. But one of the phones I was using had been this Samsung Galaxy running Android 4.4.4 (SDK API level 19) and the AOSP keyboard’s minimum API level had been 21 at the time. This having the same look as AOSP and being born from it made a good choice.

One of the best things about this keyboard is that it is what it says purports to be. Absence of two things immediately stand out when it comes to this keyboard: a spell checker and an emoji picker.

Around the time I was trying this out, I had developed this tendency to overlook making typos and just tapping on the correct spelling and the awareness of it is what led me to turning off this function altogether. While this and next word prediction may be a useful feature for many, popular word processors already come equipped with more richer text checking capability and for a lot of people the absence of this feature, in my opinion, be a good thing given that people might start paying more attention to what they type in that case.

The second thing is the emoji picker which might be a concern for the younger generation and I must say, the lack of emoji picker does affect how I communicate with people but I personally do not use emojis that much and the handful of them I use I just copy those from Telegram or WhatsApp app or after doing a websearch and that has not dissuaded me from using this keyboard as my default and only system keyboard on my phone(s).

The last thing to note and which can be a deal breaker for some is the availability of themes. The theming support on the app currently more limited than it is on the AOSP Keyboard and this bothered be a bit in the beginning but then I quickly got used to it. This app now also lacks Google’s Material Design 3 support.

Overall, I am quite satisfied with it and would 100% recommend this keyboard to anyone who just wants a simple yet reliable keyboard for their AOSP-powered phone.
