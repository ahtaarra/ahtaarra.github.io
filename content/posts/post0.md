Simple Keyboard is a keyboard app for Android OS based on the AOSP Keyboard. It is available on both the [F-Droid repository](https://f-droid.org/packages/rkr.simplekeyboard.inputmethod/) and the [Google Play Store](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=rkr.simplekeyboard.inputmethod). And needed, its APK can be directly grabbed from the project's Github releases page [here](https://github.com/rkkr/simple-keyboard/releases/).

One of the reasons I switched to this keyboard had been that at the time I used to use [Hacker's Keyboard](https://github.com/klausw/hackerskeyboard) and wanted to try out something else. But one of the phones I had been using was this Samsung Galaxy running Android 4.4.4 (SDK API level 19) and the AOSP keyboard's minimum API level had been 21 at the time. This having the same look as AOSP and being born from it made a good choice.

One of the best things about this keyboard is that it is what it says purports to be. Absence of two things immediately stand out when it comes to this keyboard: a spell checker and an emoji picker.

Around the time I was trying this out, I had developed this tendency to make typos and simply tapping on the correct spelling withough giving it a lot of thought. The awareness of it is what led me to turn off this function on keyboard apps altogether. While this and next word prediction feature may be useful for many, popular word processors already come equipped with richer text checking capabilities and for a lot of people the absence of this feature, in my opinion, can be a good thing given that people might start paying more attention to what they type due to this.

The second thing is the emoji picker which might be a concern for the younger generation in particular and I must say, the lack of emoji picker (and easy emoticon picker) does affect how I communicate with people but I personally do not use emojis a lot and the handful of those that I do use I just copy them from Telegram or WhatsApp app's pickers or getting it from the web. But this has not dissuaded me from using this as the default keyboard on my phone(s).

The last thing to note and which can be a deal breaker for some is the availability of themes. The theming support on the app currently more limited than it is on the AOSP Keyboard and this bothered be a bit in the beginning but then I quickly got used to it. This app also lacks Google's Material Design 3 support.

Overall, I am quite happy with it and would 100% recommend this to anyone who just wants a simple yet reliable keyboard for their AOSP-powered phone.
